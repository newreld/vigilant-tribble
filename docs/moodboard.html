ART DIRECTION · v1

COSMIC MERGE

A merge game drawn in the language of 1970s NASA mission posters and mid-century space graphics: warm ink, marigold + dusty teal, cream paper, flat fills with print grain. The opposite of the default "neon-on-navy, glassy spheres" space game. The point of view is the differentiation — a budget can't buy taste.

01 — PALETTE

Warm ink, one marigold, one teal

One dominant accent (marigold), one secondary (teal), brick reserved strictly for danger. Everything sits on warm near-black ink, never blue-black. Cream — not white — is the text color. Low saturation in the chrome; let the celestial bodies carry the color.

02 — TYPE

Big Shoulders Display + IBM Plex Mono

A condensed display face for identity, a clean mono for all data — deliberately not Space Grotesk or Inter (the AI-default fonts). Commit to extremes: heavy display weights against a light mono, with big size jumps. Always tabular numerals for SCORE / BEST.

03 — SIGNATURE MOTIF

The orbital ring

One recurring mark, used everywhere: a thin marigold ring with a single teal body on its orbit. It's the wordmark bullet, the next-piece frame, and the game-over flourish. A single motif repeated consistently is what people remember — so it appears at every scale rather than as decoration in one place.

04 — CELESTIAL BODIES

Flat poster planets, not glassy spheres

The bodies are the #1 brand surface. One rendering language across all nine tiers: flat fills, hard-edged bands and rings, stippled craters, paper grain — no sphere-normal gradients, no bloom. Each tier has a distinct silhouette and hue so it's readable at a glance. All rendered by one pure pixel shader (shadeBody()), shared by the game and these previews so they never drift.

05 — RULES

Do & don't

DO

  • Flat fills + print grain. Matter, not gradients.
  • 1px solid borders, 8–12px radii, shadows under 0 2px 8px.
  • Intentional asymmetry — wordmark hard-left, data clustered right.
  • Snappy, mechanical motion. Pop and settle, no float.
  • Let the bodies carry saturation; keep the chrome quiet.

DON'T

  • Glassmorphism / backdrop-blur, glow haze, big soft shadows.
  • Neon cyan + gold on navy — the default-dark-mode cliché.
  • Space Grotesk / Inter; emoji as iconography.
  • Elastic / bouncy overshoot easing on UI.
  • Perfectly symmetric, evenly-spaced, opinion-free layout.
